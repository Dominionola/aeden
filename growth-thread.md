continuously learns from user behavior to refine predictions.

### **Phase 1: The Incubation (0–30 Minutes)**

**What Happens?**

Your post is first published to your "Following" feed (a subset of your followers, not all). 

Then, to a small "test cohort" of non-followers based on interest-graph matching.

**What The Algorithm Measures?**

- **First Hour Velocity**: Engagement rate within the first 15–60 minutes
- **Interaction Quality**: Are people replying, or just scrolling past?
- **Signal Strength**: Reply length, profile taps, dwell time (time spent on your post)

**Critical Threshold:**

- If **5–10% of the initial test group** engages (specifically via replies or reposts), the post graduates to Phase 2.
- If engagement is weak, the post remains visible to only a fraction of your followers and dies.

**Why Most Posts Die Here:**

- Weak hook that doesn't stop the scroll
- No reply trigger embedded in your post
- Posted during low-activity windows

**The Golden Window Protocol**

Immediately after posting, execute:

1. **Reply to your own post** (if it's a thread, add context immediately)
2. **Stay in-app**: Reply to the first 3 comments within 60 seconds
3. **Engage with 3–5 posts in your niche** to trigger activity signals

This artificially inflates velocity during the critical evaluation window.

---

### **Phase 2: The Network Ripple (30 Minutes – 4 Hours)**

**What Happens?**

Replies from Phase 1 push your post into **indirect reach**.

When User A replies to your post, your post appears in User A's followers' feeds with the context: *"User A replied to this."*

This is the "social proof expansion layer."

**What The Algorithm Measures?**

- **Reply cascades**: Are replies generating more replies?
- **Conversation topology**: Hub-and-spoke (broadcasting) vs. chain-style (dialogue)
- **Re-engagement frequency**: Are people returning to the thread?

Social Media Engagement Patterns
1. Hub-and-Spoke
Type: Broadcasting Pattern

Description: One author post followed by many unconnected comments.

Signal: This pattern signals broad appeal but low community depth.

2. The Chain
Type: Algorithm Favorite

Description: One author post with deep reply threads between users.

Signal: This signals high community depth and MSI (Meaningful Social Interaction).

Note: The algorithm strongly favors chains.

3. The Web
Type: Viral Spread Pattern

Description: High volume of reposts and quote-posts.

Signal: This signals that the content is a "carrier" of identity, prompting users to share it.

**The Reply Ladder Effect**

By replying to comments with **questions** (not just "thanks!"), you:

- Double notification density (commenter gets alerted again)
- Keep the post "active" in timestamp hierarchies
- Signal to the algorithm that the conversation is ongoing, not concluded

**Example:**

❌ **Low-Value Reply**: "Thanks for sharing!"

✅ **High-Value Reply**: "That's a great point, how do you balance that with [specific constraint]?"

The latter invites a response, extending thread depth and inflating the MSI score.

**Why Phase 2 Determines Virality?**

If your content generates **conversation chains** (back-and-forth between multiple users), it signals high community engagement.

The algorithm interprets this as: *"This content is facilitating meaningful connection."*

That's the unlock signal for Phase 3.

---

### **Phase 3: The "For You" Injection (4 Hours – 48 Hours)**

**What Happens?**

Having passed velocity and network checks, the algorithm injects the post into the **"For You" feeds** of non-followers.

**Targeting Logic:**

The system uses **semantic matching** and **collaborative filtering**:

- Analyzes the text of your post to extract topics
- Identifies users with similar engagement histories to your initial engagers
- Tests the post with "lookalike audiences" who don't follow you yet

**This Is Where 90% of Impressions Occur.**

**What The Algorithm Measures?**

- **Cross-audience engagement**: Do strangers reply as much as followers?
- **Conversion signals**: Profile taps, follows, saves
- **Negative signals**: Hides, mutes, blocks (instant suppression)

**Peak Virality Window**

Most viral posts experience their largest spike between **6–24 hours** after posting. If the post continues generating engagement beyond 24 hours, it enters extended distribution cycles.

**The Lookalike Problem (Why You Can't Control Everything):**

You cannot control who the algorithm selects as your initial test cohort. 

If the wrong lookalike group sees your post first, it may flop despite being objectively strong content. This is the irreducible variance in the system.

**But You Can Stack The Odds**

By consistently posting in a **narrow topic vertical (niche)**, you train the algorithm to identify your niche more accurately, improving lookalike precision over time.

---

Content is indexed in Search results and "Trending Now" topics.

**Survival Criteria:**

Only posts with **high save rates** or **quotes** typically survive here:

- Tutorials
- Resource lists
- Data-backed insights
- Frameworks and mental models

## The Safety Layer (How To Avoid Suppression)

Meta operates aggressive content integrity systems designed to downrank low-quality or manipulative content.

Understanding suppression triggers is critical to avoid **shadowbanning** (reach restriction without notification).

### Confirmed Suppression Triggers:

**1. Engagement Bait**

Explicitly asking for likes, shares, or follows.

Examples:

- ❌ "Like this if you agree"
- ❌ "Tag someone who needs this"
- ❌ "Follow for more"

**Why It's Penalized:**

Text classifiers identify these as spam patterns.

---

**2. Rapid-Fire Posting**

Posting 5+ times within 10 minutes triggers anti-spam filters.

**Why It's Penalized:**

The system flags the account as bot-like behavior.

</aside>

**3. Keyword/Hashtag Stuffing**

Using 10+ irrelevant hashtags or cramming keywords unnaturally.

**Why It's Penalized:**

Decreases semantic clarity, making the post harder to categorize accurately.

---

**4. Recycled Content**

Exact text matches from **other users** or your **own previous posts.**

**Why It's Penalized:**

Flagged as unoriginal, capping distribution.

---

**5. "Rage Bait"**

Content that generates high replies but also high hide/mute/block rates.

**Why It's Penalized:**

Signals toxicity. Meta prioritizes ecosystem health over raw engagement.

---

### The Safety Checklist (Run Before Publishing):

- [ ]  Does the post explicitly ask for engagement? (Remove)
- [ ]  Have I posted 3+ times in the last hour? (Wait)
- [ ]  Am I using 5+ hashtags? (Reduce to 1–2 max)
- [ ]  Is this text copied from elsewhere? (Rewrite)
- [ ]  Will this make people angry enough to mute me? (Reconsider tone)

**Score 5/5 → Safe to post**
